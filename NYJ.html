Born A Crime

Born a Crime is a South African-inspired book written by Trevor Noah. The Title Born a Crime is drawn to the fact that
he was born to a white father and black mother which was illegal under apartheid laws.
The main theme discussed in chapter one is racial discrimination and the challenges of people who come
from mixed races are also experienced in Nairobi.


Education and Job Opportunities
Nairobi, just like South Africa experiences instances where one has job opportunities and education opportunities as a result
of how they look.
White people are given priority in some restaurants. They are also served the best food and the rest of the food is served to blacks.

Moreover, Trevor Noah's search for identity and looking for a sense of belonging is similar to that of Kenya
because Kenya is home to many ethnic groups. Each ethnic group has its own culture that is unique and with a different history.
Trevor Noah's search for identity is similar to many people who struggle to fit in and
search for their origin. Various people lose job opportunities because they come from specific ethnic groups.
Trevor Noah's mother was given the lowest-ranked job in the office because she was black and the whites were
given nice and high-paying jobs.


In conclusion, the theme of discrimination based on ethnicity and education is prevalent in Kenya and is similar
to that of South Africa whereby the whites had taken the country and had total control over the people.
The whites used to receive the best education and also get the best jobs leaving the Africans with nothing but the
bare minimum. Nairobi and South Africa face similar social issues and are all trying to equalize everyone.
